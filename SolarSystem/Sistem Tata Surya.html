  1. S U N

    2. The sun is an important member in the solar system which is the main component in solar system. The sun is also referred to as the parent in the solar system. The sun has 332,830 Earth masses in size. By having this large mass size giving rise to a large core density in order to support the continuity of nuclear fusion and generates a tremendous amount of energy.

    Then this energy is emitted into space electromagnetic radiation and includes magnetic spectrum. The sun also has layers, namely:

    • The core is the innermost layer of the sun temperature of 14 million kelvin, and these cores are where nuclear reactions occur which will produce a very large amount of energy.
    • The photosphere is the layer that still has tremendous heat energy at its temperature about 6000 kelvin which has a thickness of about 300 km. And the photosphere is part of the sun that can be seen, but we can not always look at the sun directly as it may cause eye damage.
    • The chromosphere is the layer of the sun's atmosphere which has a temperature of 4,500 kelvin and has a thickness of 2,000 kilometers.
    • The corona is the outer layer of the sun that doesn't give off much heat energy because the corona has a temperature of 1 million kelvin and has a thickness of 700,000 km. The heat energy that is always emitted by the sun allows life to exist on Earth earth. And without the reflection of sunlight. So we can't see the other members of the solar system only look like stars, meteors and comets.
